  • You have to be an English Native Speaker.

Informed Consent

This is a linguistic experiment performed at the University of Pennsylvania. If you have any question about this study, feel free to contact Dr. Chris Callison-Burch. Participation in this research is voluntary. You have the right to withdraw from the experiment at any time. The collected data will be used for research purposes only. Personal data will be kept confidential and will not be shared with third parties.

Instructions

In this task you will read part of a conversation. You will see a series of prompts, and possible responses to these prompts created by a computer program. The program attemp$ s to generate responses that are relevant, while also making an interesting contribution to the conversation.

For each prompt, you will read five possible responses, and judge the quality of each response on a scale from 1 to 3. The descriptions of these levels are as follows:

  1. The response is ungrammatical or incoherent.
  2. The response is basically grammatical, but is either irrelevant to the input prompt, or is not interesting enough to continue the conversation.
  3. The response is grammatical, relevant to the input prompt, and is an interesting contribution to the conversation.

Example: Below we show a prompt and several possible responses, along with a suggested score for each response.
